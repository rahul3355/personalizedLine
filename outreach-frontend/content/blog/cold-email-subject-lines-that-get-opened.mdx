## The Psychology Behind High-Performing Subject Lines

Before we dive into the templates, you need to understand WHY certain subject lines work.

### Principle 1: Curiosity Gap

People can't resist closing information gaps. The best subject lines tease value without revealing everything.

**Example:**
- ‚ùå "How to Get More Sales Leads"
- ‚úÖ "The lead gen strategy everyone's copying"

The first is boring and expected. The second creates mystery.

### Principle 2: Personalization (But Not Obvious)

Using {{FirstName}} in subject lines is played out. Modern personalization references:

- Their company
- Their role
- Their recent activity
- Industry-specific language

**Example:**
- ‚ùå "John, can we talk?"
- ‚úÖ "Re: Acme Corp's Q2 hiring push"

### Principle 3: Brevity Wins

Subject lines with **3-5 words** get opened 21% more than longer ones.

Why? They stand out in crowded inboxes and don't get truncated on mobile.

### Principle 4: No Spam Trigger Words

Avoid these like the plague:
- Free
- Opportunity
- Limited time
- Special offer
- Click here
- Act now
- Reminder

They scream "salesperson" and trigger spam filters.

## Subject Line Testing Framework

Don't just copy-paste these. Test systematically:

### Step 1: Pick Your Control

Choose one subject line as your baseline. Send it to 20-30% of your list.

### Step 2: Test Variations

Test one variable at a time:
- Length (3 words vs 5 words)
- Question vs statement
- Personalized vs generic
- Curiosity vs value prop

### Step 3: Measure What Matters

Track:
- **Open rate** (obvious)
- **Reply rate** (more important)
- **Spam complaints** (critical for deliverability)
- **Unsubscribe rate** (health check)

### Step 4: Scale What Works

Once a subject line beats your control by 15%+, make it the new baseline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I use emojis in subject lines?

**Test them.** Some audiences love them (B2C, younger demographics). Others hate them (enterprise buyers, executives).

Data shows 1 emoji can increase opens by 15-20% in the right context. More than 1 usually decreases opens.

### How long should my subject line be?

**3-5 words is the sweet spot** for cold email. Longer subject lines work for newsletters or content, but cold outreach demands brevity.

### Should I personalize every subject line?

**Not necessarily.** Personalization helps when it's:
- Genuinely relevant
- Not obvious (avoid {{FirstName}})
- Based on research, not assumptions

Generic subject lines can outperform bad personalization.

### How often should I test new subject lines?

**Every campaign.** Set up a perpetual A/B test:
- 50% of sends use your control
- 25% test variant A
- 25% test variant B

Winners become new controls.

### Do subject lines matter more than email body?

**Both matter.** A great subject line gets opens. A great email body gets replies.

Open rate without reply rate is vanity metrics.
